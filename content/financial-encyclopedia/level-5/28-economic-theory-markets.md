# Chapter 28: Economic Theory & Markets

## 28.1 Macroeconomic Indicators
Macroeconomic indicators are high-level data points used to gauge the health of an economy.
* **Leading Indicators:** Move before the overall economy (e.g., building permits, yield curve).
* **Coincident Indicators:** Move with the economy (e.g., employment levels, industrial production).
* **Lagging Indicators:** Confirmed after a trend (e.g., GDP, CPI/Inflation).



## 28.2 Monetary vs. Fiscal Policy
* **Monetary Policy:** Controlled by the Federal Reserve; involves interest rates and money supply management.
* **Fiscal Policy:** Controlled by the Government; involves taxation and federal spending.

# Chapter 22: Quantitative Finance

## 22.1 Financial Modeling
[cite_start]Quantitative finance uses mathematical models to forecast performance and value assets. Key components include Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) techniques and sensitivity analysis to test how results change with variations in interest rates or growth[cite: 22].

## 22.2 Monte Carlo Simulations
[cite_start]A statistical technique that models thousands of random scenarios to define the probability of different financial outcomes[cite: 22].


## 22.3 Risk Parity
[cite_start]Risk parity allocates capital based on risk contribution rather than dollar amount, ensuring balanced volatility across asset classes[cite: 22].
